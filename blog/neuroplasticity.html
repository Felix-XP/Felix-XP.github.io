Neuroplasticity

What is Neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity refers to the physiological aspects of the brain, as an organ, and the chemical processes that takes place. Neuroplasticity compares the physical properties of the brain to that of 'plastic'. Neuroplasticity suggest that the brain physiological attributes can be altered over time.
Neuroplasiticty is an idea drawn from the feild of nueroscience. Neuroscience is the biological/physical representation of thoughts and emotions. Neuroplasiticty discusses the cellular interactivity of neurons, the creation and deterioration processes of nueropathways and how you can manipulate these chemical processes using your thoughts and/or mental exercises.
This is a particularly good 'concept' for people to know. Alot of studies as have claimed that simply knowing that you can change your brain structure, in itself, increases neuroplasticity and brain functionality. It can be quite the motivating factor for people to understand that your thoughts and ideas are not fixed. Neuroplasity essentially follows the 'use it or loose it' rule in any attempts to optimise on nueroplasticity. Initiating increased practice and focus on a particular habit, idea or task, given enough time, will reallocate brain function to support your focusses and that once difficult task you had, becomes so much easier. This process is essentially Neuroplasticity in action.

Growth Mindet

Growth mindset refers to the behaviours and attitudes you can adopt in your general appraoch to learning and life. Having a growth mindset means to silmultaneously acknowledge your limits, but also more importantly, your potential for development. This means that a more acurate assessment of your capabilites should include not just your current acheivement level, but also acheivement possibilities. Without scale(the consideration of time), 'growth mindset' suggest an endless learning and acheivement possibility. The determining factor of your success, in whatever context, would be repetition (practice).
Whilst the idea of 'growth mindest' exist in a more abstract sense, along with the idea of neuroplasiticity, we can recognise a more fuller and deeper understanding of this idea, how we can use it for our benefit.
