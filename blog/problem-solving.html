Problem Solving Techniques

Below are a list of problem solving techniques and how I fell utilising them:
  • I like using Pseudocode because I'm still quite old fashioned with my pen and paper. It also good because I can interpret the steps or ideas I need, quite well because I'm using my own language. Its a good intiate process for probelm solving.
  • At this point I don't like the technique of Trying something. I do use it though, its just everything I try, doesnt work.
  • The Rubber Ducky method is a funny one for me cause halfway through I realise im talking to an inanimate object, then I start I start talking to myself, then it gets weird.
  • I like reading error messages cause they provide a lot of useful information
  • I use console logging quite a bit to see if my code runs and gives me the output I expect.
  • Not a big fan of googling. Sometimes there is just too much information I am reading through and find it hard to disern what is useful.
  • Asking people for help is not a strong suit of mine let alone asking my peers for help.
  • I'm algoods asking my facilitator for help because they are quite smart and I know they are always willing to help.
  • I'm big on using reflection as a porblem solving technique. It helps me pinpoint where I went wrong and think of alternative solutions for that past scenario. Those alternative solutions have a high possiblity of being soltuions for future problems.

I think the problem I have with 'asking for help' might be linked to two things. One, that I've lived a life uptill now that has required me to solely rely on myself and this old habit as made its way into my core beliefs and Two; past traumas from the times I did ask for help and it ended up backfiring. At this point in my life I am coming to realise the importance of family, companionship and friendship more deeply. With a little bit of courage, utilising on the people around can greatly serve you in your life. So for me, I need more courage, and this is something that can come from practicing the art of 'reaching out'.
